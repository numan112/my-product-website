Ingredient 01

NMN — Nicotinamide Mononucleotide

NMN is a direct precursor to NAD+, one of the most studied molecules in longevity research. The science behind NAD+ decline and restoration is among the most robust in the aging field.

The NAD+ decline problem

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is essential for mitochondrial energy production, DNA damage repair via sirtuins and PARP enzymes, and cellular metabolism. By age 40, NAD+ levels have typically fallen 40–50% compared to young adulthood. By 60, the decline can reach 80%.

This decline correlates with virtually every hallmark of aging — reduced energy, impaired DNA repair, mitochondrial dysfunction, and metabolic decline. Restoring NAD+ levels is one of the most studied interventions in longevity research.

NMN enters cells directly via the Slc12a8 transporter and is rapidly converted to NAD+. Multiple human clinical trials have demonstrated that oral NMN supplementation raises blood NAD+ levels significantly, with the most consistent results at 500mg daily.

Ingredient 02

Magnesium Glycinate — 120mg elemental

Magnesium is the fourth most abundant mineral in the body and a cofactor in over 300 enzymatic reactions. Yet most adults are chronically deficient — and most magnesium supplements are poorly absorbed.

Why deficiency is the norm

Modern agricultural practices have depleted soil magnesium significantly over the past century. Combined with diets high in processed foods and low in leafy greens, nuts, and seeds, studies estimate 50–80% of adults in developed countries do not meet the recommended daily intake.

Magnesium is required for ATP synthesis (every energy-producing reaction in the body), DNA replication and repair, neurotransmitter regulation, muscle contraction and relaxation, and blood glucose regulation. Chronic deficiency is associated with poor sleep, muscle cramps, anxiety, and increased cardiovascular risk.

We use glycinate specifically because magnesium oxide — the most common form — has approximately 4% bioavailability. Glycinate chelation binds magnesium to glycine for dramatically superior absorption without gastrointestinal side effects.

Ingredient 03

Vitamin D3 — 5,000 IU

Vitamin D3 is not a vitamin in the conventional sense. It is a secosteroid hormone with receptor sites in virtually every tissue in the human body. Over 40% of adults are clinically deficient.

A hormone, not just a vitamin

Vitamin D receptors (VDR) have been identified in over 30 different tissue types including the brain, heart, immune cells, and reproductive organs. D3 regulates over 200 genes involved in immune function, cell proliferation, and inflammation.

Deficiency is defined as serum 25(OH)D below 20 ng/mL. Insufficiency is below 30 ng/mL. Most longevity researchers and clinicians aim for 50–80 ng/mL. Achieving this in deficient individuals typically requires 2,000–5,000 IU daily — not the 400–800 IU found in most multivitamins.

The 400 IU RDA was established to prevent rickets — not to optimize immune function, mood, cardiovascular health, or longevity. We dose for optimization, not minimum adequacy.

Ingredient 04

Vitamin K2 MK-7 — 100mcg

K2 is the ingredient most people taking D3 are missing — and the most important one for long-term cardiovascular safety when supplementing with high-dose D3.

The calcium traffic director

Vitamin K2 activates two critical proteins through a process called carboxylation. Osteocalcin, produced by osteoblasts in bone, requires K2 activation to bind calcium into bone matrix. Matrix Gla Protein (MGP), found in arterial walls, requires K2 to inhibit calcium deposition in arteries.

When D3 is supplemented without K2, calcium absorption from the gut increases significantly. Without active MGP to direct that calcium away from arteries, it can deposit in arterial walls — accelerating arterial calcification and cardiovascular risk.

MK-7 (menaquinone-7) is derived from natto fermentation and is the most bioavailable, longest-acting form of K2. Its half-life of 72 hours (vs 1–2 hours for MK-4) ensures sustained carboxylation activity throughout the day from a single daily dose.

Magnesium + D3

Activation dependency

Magnesium is required to convert vitamin D3 into its active hormonal form (1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D). In magnesium-deficient individuals, D3 supplementation may be less effective — or may deplete magnesium stores further. Combined supplementation addresses both issues simultaneously.
