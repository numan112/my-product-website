The Formulation

Every ingredient. Every reason.

You take your health seriously. Here is exactly why each ingredient is in this formula and why the dose matters.

01
NMN
500mg
Why it's here

Nicotinamide Mononucleotide

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is the coenzyme at the center of cellular energy production, DNA repair, and mitochondrial function. By age 50, NAD+ levels have typically declined by 50% compared to young adulthood. NMN is the most efficient direct precursor to NAD+ biosynthesis.

At 500mg, this is a clinically studied dose. Lower doses (100–250mg) are commonly used in cheaper products and are unlikely to produce meaningful NAD+ elevation in most adults.

02
Mg Glyc.
120mg
Why it's here

Magnesium Glycinate

Magnesium is a cofactor in over 300 enzymatic reactions including ATP synthesis, DNA replication, and protein synthesis. Studies estimate 50–80% of adults do not meet adequate daily intake. Most people who think they take magnesium are taking oxide — a form with roughly 4% bioavailability.

Glycinate chelation binds magnesium to the amino acid glycine, dramatically improving absorption and bioavailability without the laxative effect of citrate or oxide forms. At 120mg elemental, this is a therapeutic dose for sleep quality, muscle recovery, and neurological function.

03
D3
5,000 IU
Why it's here

Vitamin D3 — Cholecalciferol

Vitamin D3 functions as a steroid hormone, not merely a vitamin. VDR (vitamin D receptor) sites are found in virtually every tissue in the body. D3 regulates immune system modulation, bone mineral density, cardiovascular health, testosterone production, and mood regulation via serotonin synthesis.

The 400–800 IU found in most multivitamins is insufficient to raise serum levels from deficiency. Research consistently shows 2,000–5,000 IU is required for meaningful impact in deficient individuals. We use 5,000 IU — the dose serious researchers and clinicians actually use.

04
K2 MK-7
100mcg
Why it's here

Vitamin K2 MK-7

K2 activates two critical proteins: osteocalcin, which anchors calcium into bone matrix, and matrix Gla protein (MGP), which prevents calcium from depositing in arterial walls. High-dose D3 supplementation without K2 increases calcium absorption — if that calcium ends up in arteries rather than bones, it accelerates cardiovascular risk.

MK-7 (menaquinone-7) is the most bioavailable and longest-acting form of K2, with a half-life of 72 hours compared to MK-4's 1–2 hours. At 100mcg, this is the dose with the most supporting evidence for arterial protection and bone health.
